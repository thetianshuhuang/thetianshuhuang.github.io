I am a PhD student co-advised by Anthony Rowe and Carlee Joe-Wong at Carnegie Mellon Electrical and Computer Engineering. My interests are in applying Machine Learning to Systems in ways that leverage both data-driven learning techniques and a theoretical and intuitive understanding of the properties of systems.

Previously, I obtained my B.S. in Electrical Engineering at the University of Texas at Austin in May 2021. While at UT Austin, I worked on Learning to Optimize research with Atlas Wang.

Please contact me at tianshu@cmu.edu. You can also find my GitHub page here.

Current Research

  • Resource Prediction for Distributed Systems (Fall 2021-)
    The SilverLine framework is a system for executing WebAssembly modules in a heterogeneous distributed environment.
    In this project, our goal is to build a resource usage predictor and allocator for SilverLine, and for heterogeneous distributed systems in general.
  • Applications for Neural Radiance Fields (Spring 2022-)
    Since its introduction in 2020, Neural Radiance Fields (NeRFs) have garnered increasing attention. Several order of magnitude improvements have shifted NeRFs from a mere toy problem to a technique which will soon have convincing applications.
    Applications I am interested in include practical space scanning, real time reality capture, and applying NeRF approaches to non-graphics problems.
